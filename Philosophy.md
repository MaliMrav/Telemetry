# Philosophy of Systems Engineering

## 1. Engineering is a conversation with reality

A system never behaves exactly as designed.

It behaves as physics, constraints, and interactions allow.

Good engineering is listening to that behaviour.

---

## 2. Complexity is inevitable. Confusion is optional.

Systems will become complex.

Our job is not to prevent complexity.

Our job is to ensure it remains understandable.

---

## 3. Observability is more important than correctness

A correct system that cannot be observed is indistinguishable from a broken one.

Telemetry is not diagnostic—it is foundational.

---

## 4. State is the truth

Most system failures are state misunderstandings.

Not code bugs.

Not hardware faults.

State mismatches.

---

## 5. UX is an architectural concern

User experience is not a layer on top of the system.

It is the system as perceived by humans.

Provisioning flows, error states, and recovery paths are architectural decisions.

---

## 6. Abstractions are contracts with future confusion

Every abstraction:
- hides something
- delays understanding
- introduces coupling

Use them deliberately, not automatically.

---

## 7. Systems are discovered, not designed

No non-trivial system is fully correct on first design.

Systems emerge through:
- experimentation
- measurement
- failure
- refinement

---

## 8. Documentation is part of the system

If it is not documented, it does not exist for the next engineer.

If it is not understandable, it is not maintainable.

---

## 9. Engineering is teaching

The best engineers are not those who build the most complex systems.

They are those who can explain them clearly.

---

## 10. Curiosity is a design tool

Every "why does this happen?" is a potential system improvement.

Curiosity is not overhead.

It is a debugging interface for reality.
